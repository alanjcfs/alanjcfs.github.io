I got a cochlear implant in August 2020 and was activated in September. This was my first significant surgery.

This was after 36 years of being deaf, and 32 years of wearing hearing aids. I went through speech therapy. The outcome of that was that I can speak better than I can hear. I decided after thinking about it for a long time, and after being in the Deaf community, learning ASL when I was 17, that getting the implant was an important step toward self-improvement.

The implant — even though it allowed me to hear quiet sound, like hearing a crinkling bag in a grocery story from far away — wasn’t a panacea. It turns out that my brain had a lot of work it needed to do to comprehend speech and this is a long ongoing process.
